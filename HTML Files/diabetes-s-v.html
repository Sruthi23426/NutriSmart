NutriSmart

About Diabetes

South Indian diets are often rice-heavy, which may raise blood sugar quickly. Switching to millets, increasing fiber intake, and balancing meals with protein helps maintain stable glucose levels.

Foods to Include ✅

  • Millets (ragi, foxtail, little millet)
  • Brown rice (controlled portions)
  • Sambar (lentils + vegetables)
  • Vegetable poriyal (low oil)
  • Buttermilk and curd
  • Leafy greens (keerai)

Foods to Avoid ❌

  • Large portions of white rice
  • Fried snacks like vada, bajji
  • Sugary sweets (payasam, kesari)
  • Refined flour items

Diet Plan Structure 🍽️

  • Breakfast Options: Ragi dosa / idli (2) / oats upma / vegetable pongal (light)
  • Mid-Morning: Guava / coconut water / small fruit bowl
  • Lunch Options: Brown rice (small portion) + sambar + poriyal + salad
  • Evening Snack: Sundal / sprouts / buttermilk
  • Dinner Options: Vegetable soup / light dosa / sautéed vegetables
  • Optional: Jeera water / herbal drinks

Recommended Exercises 🏃

  • Brisk walking (30 min)
  • Yoga
  • Light strength training

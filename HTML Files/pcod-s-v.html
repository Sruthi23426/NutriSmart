About PCOD

Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is a hormonal condition often linked with insulin resistance and irregular metabolism.South Indian diets rich in fermented foods and millets can help improve gut health and hormonal balance when properly balanced.

Foods to Include ✅

  • Ragi, millet-based foods
  • Idli/dosa (fermented)
  • Vegetables and sambar
  • Buttermilk
  • Sprouts and legumes

Foods to Avoid ❌

  • Sugary foods and desserts
  • Refined flour (maida items)
  • Fried snacks
  • Packaged foods
  • High sugar beverages

Flagged Foods ⚠️

Excess dairy and high-carb meals can worsen insulin resistance. Balance carbs with protein.

Diet Plan Structure 🍽️

  • Breakfast: Ragi dosa / idli / oats upma
  • Mid-Morning: Coconut water / fruit
  • Lunch: Millet rice + sambar + poriyal
  • Evening: Sundal / buttermilk
  • Dinner: Vegetable soup / light dosa

Why This Plan Works 📊

  • Millets have low glycemic index
  • Fermented foods improve gut health
  • Balanced meals regulate hormones

Recommended Exercises 🏃

  • Brisk walking (30–40 min)
  • Strength training (important for PCOD)
  • Yoga (hormone balancing)

About Hypertension

Hypertension (high blood pressure) is often linked to excess salt intake, stress, and low physical activity. In North Indian diets, pickles, papads, and salty snacks can contribute to increased sodium levels. Managing portion size and reducing salt is key.

Foods to Include ✅

  • Whole wheat roti (low salt)
  • Green leafy vegetables (spinach, methi)
  • Low-fat dairy (curd, buttermilk)
  • Fruits rich in potassium (banana, orange)
  • Unsalted nuts (almonds, walnuts)
  • Oats and dal

Foods to Avoid ❌

  • Pickles and papad
  • Packaged and processed foods
  • Excess salt in cooking
  • Fried snacks
  • Instant noodles and soups

Daily Diet Structure 🍽️

  • Breakfast Options: Vegetable poha / oats / dal chilla / upma
  • Mid-morning: Fruit (banana/orange) or coconut water
  • Lunch: 2 roti + dal + sabzi + salad
  • Evening Snack: Roasted chana / sprouts / buttermilk
  • Dinner: Light roti + sabzi OR vegetable soup
  • Optional: Fresh lime water (low salt)

Why This Plan Works 📊

  • Low sodium helps control blood pressure
  • High potassium foods balance sodium levels
  • Fiber improves heart health
  • Light dinner reduces nighttime strain on heart

Recommended Exercises 🏃

  • Brisk walking (30 min daily)
  • Yoga (pranayama, breathing exercises)
  • Light stretching

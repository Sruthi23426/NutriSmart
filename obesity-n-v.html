NutriSmart

About Obesity

Obesity occurs due to excess calorie intake and low physical activity. In North Indian diets, frequent consumption of wheat-based foods, ghee, and fried items can contribute to weight gain. The focus is on portion control and balanced nutrition.

Foods to Include ✅

  • Whole wheat roti (limited quantity)
  • Dal and legumes (protein source)
  • Seasonal vegetables (sabzi, salad)
  • Low-fat curd and buttermilk
  • Fruits like apple, papaya, orange
  • Sprouts and oats

Foods to Avoid ❌

  • Parathas with excess ghee/butter
  • Deep-fried snacks (pakora, samosa)
  • Sweets (jalebi, laddoo)
  • Refined flour (maida items)
  • Sugary beverages

Diet Plan Structure 🍽️

  • Breakfast Options: Oats / vegetable poha / upma / sprouts salad
  • Mid-Morning: Seasonal fruits (papaya, apple) or buttermilk
  • Lunch Options: 2 roti + dal / chole / rajma + sabzi + salad
  • Evening Snack: Roasted chana / sprouts / herbal tea
  • Dinner Options: Vegetable soup / khichdi / light sabzi + salad
  • Optional: Lemon water / jeera water

Why This Plan Works 📊

  • Controls calorie intake through portion management
  • Includes protein to reduce hunger
  • Light dinners help reduce fat storage
  • High fiber improves digestion and satiety

Recommended Exercises 🏃

  • Brisk walking (40–45 min)
  • Yoga (fat-loss focused)
  • Skipping or light cardio
  • Daily step goal (8,000+)

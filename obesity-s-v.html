About Obesity

Obesity is caused by excess calorie intake and low energy expenditure. In South Indian diets, high consumption of rice and fried foods can contribute to weight gain. The goal is to create a calorie deficit while maintaining nutrition.

Foods to Include ✅

  • Millets (ragi, jowar, foxtail)
  • Vegetable-rich dishes (sambar, poriyal)
  • High-fiber foods (oats, sprouts)
  • Low-fat curd and buttermilk
  • Fruits like papaya, apple, guava
  • Green leafy vegetables

Foods to Avoid ❌

  • White rice in large quantities
  • Fried snacks (vada, chips, bajji)
  • Sugary foods and beverages
  • Refined flour (maida-based foods)
  • Excess oil and ghee

Diet Plan Structure 🍽️

  • Breakfast Options: Ragi dosa / idli / oats upma / vegetable pongal
  • Mid-Morning: Coconut water / fruit bowl
  • Lunch Options: Brown rice / millet rice + sambar + poriyal + curd
  • Evening Snack: Sundal / buttermilk / sprouts
  • Dinner Options: Vegetable soup / light dosa / upma (small portion)
  • Optional: Herbal drinks / jeera water

Recommended Exercises 🏃

  • Brisk walking (40–45 min)
  • Skipping or light cardio
  • Yoga for fat loss
  • Daily step goal (8,000–10,000)

Health Tip 💡

Small calorie deficits consistently lead to sustainable weight loss. Avoid crash diets.

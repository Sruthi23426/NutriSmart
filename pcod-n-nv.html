About PCOD

Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is a hormonal condition often linked with insulin resistance and irregular metabolism. A high-fiber, low glycemic index vegetarian diet can help regulate hormones and improve insulin sensitivity.

Foods to Include ✅

  • Whole grains (roti, oats, quinoa)
  • Protein-rich foods (paneer, dal, sprouts)
  • Leafy vegetables (spinach, methi)
  • Seeds (flaxseeds, chia seeds)
  • Low-fat dairy
  • Fruits with low GI (apple, berries)

Foods to Include ✅

  • Grilled chicken
  • Boiled eggs
  • Fish
  • Whole grains
  • Vegetables
  • Seeds and nuts

Flagged Foods ⚠️

Excess dairy and high-carb meals can worsen insulin resistance. Balance carbs with protein.

Diet Plan Structure 🍽️

  • Breakfast: Eggs + oats / omelette
  • Mid-Morning: Fruit + nuts
  • Lunch: Roti + chicken/fish + sabzi
  • Evening: Sprouts / roasted snacks
  • Dinner: Grilled protein + vegetables

Why This Plan Works 📊

  • Lean protein improves insulin response
  • Reduces cravings and overeating
  • Supports muscle and metabolism

Recommended Exercises 🏃

  • Brisk walking (30–40 min)
  • Strength training (important for PCOD)
  • Yoga (hormone balancing)

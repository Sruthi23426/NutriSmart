About PCOS

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder associated with insulin resistance, inflammation, and irregular menstrual cycles. Managing diet is essential to stabilize blood sugar levels and support hormonal balance.

Foods to Include ✅

  • Whole grains (oats, whole wheat roti, quinoa)
  • High-fiber vegetables
  • Plant protein (dal, chickpeas, sprouts)
  • Healthy fats (nuts, seeds)
  • Low GI fruits (berries, apple)

Foods to Avoid ❌

  • Refined carbs (maida, white bread)
  • Sugary drinks and sweets
  • Fried foods
  • Highly processed snacks

Flagged Foods ⚠️

High-carb meals without protein can spike insulin levels. Always combine carbohydrates with protein or fiber.

Diet Plan Structure 🍽️

  • Breakfast: Oats + seeds / sprouts salad / besan chilla
  • Mid-Morning: Fruit + nuts
  • Lunch: 1–2 roti + dal + sabzi + salad
  • Evening: Roasted chana / herbal tea
  • Dinner: Vegetable soup / paneer + vegetables
  • Optional: Cinnamon water / herbal tea

Why This Plan Works 📊

  • Stabilizes insulin levels
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Improves hormonal balance
  • Prevents sudden cravings

Recommended Exercises 🏃

  • Strength training (very important)
  • Brisk walking
  • Yoga (hormone balancing)
